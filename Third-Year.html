MAT389H1: Complex Analysis
Taught by Professor Tristan Collins
Fall 2024
Lecture and Tutorial Notes: PDF
Problem Set Solutions: HW 1 | HW 2 | HW 3 | HW 4 | HW 5 | HW 6 | HW 7 | HW 8 |

This course covers complex analysis and its applications. Key topics include analytic functions, the Cauchy-Riemann equations, contour integration, Cauchy's theorem, Taylor and Laurent series, singularities, and residue calculus. It also explores conformal mapping, harmonic functions, Dirichlet and Neumann boundary value problems, and Poisson integral formulas. The course extends to linear differential equations in the complex plane, focusing on Bessel and Legendre functions. Note - no homework was assigned for week 6
